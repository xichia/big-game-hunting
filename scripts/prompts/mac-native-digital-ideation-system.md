## Why this pilot exists

A previous bulk ideation pass (`artifacts/research/0002`–`0055`, synthesized in `artifacts/synthesis/0001-bulk-raw-ideation-synthesis.md` and reviewed in `artifacts/reviews/0001-bulk-raw-ideation-synthesis-review.md`) produced concepts that were mechanically clever but weakly compelling: abstract, tabletop-style social-deduction and token games with hand-wavy "reading the room" mastery claims. The human has now added a hard constraint to test: **the game must be programmable by Ian, developed on Mac, and runnable natively on Mac.** Your job is to generate candidates squarely inside that constraint — concrete Mac-native digital games, not tabletop games ported to a screen and not generic abstract mechanisms.

## What you must NOT do

- Do not propose tabletop, board, card, or party games described as if they merely "could" be digital. The candidate must be conceived as software from the start.
- Do not claim any candidate "is the winner," "should be built," or is official. Use exploratory, hedged language throughout.
- Do not use source-of-truth language (no "ratified," no declaring this is now the project's direction, no implying `GAME_TRUTH.md` has changed).
- Do not propose MMO, online-only, live-service, open-world, content-heavy, or art-heavy concepts.
- Do not propose concepts whose only depth is subjective social adjudication or debate.
- Avoid abstract token/bidding mechanisms unless going digital clearly makes them a stronger experience (computation, hidden state, animation, real-time pressure, procedural variation).
- Do not imply any working title is final branding.
- Prefer concrete player experience and visible tension over elegant-but-inert mechanism.

## Field guidance

- **Core loop**: describe 30-90 seconds of actual play, concretely — not a mechanic description.
- **First five minutes**: what a beginner actually does first, and why it is immediately understandable without explanation.
- **Native-digital reason**: name the specific digital ingredient this candidate needs — computation, hidden state, animation, simulation, procedural generation, real-time interaction, AI behavior, physics, or UI feedback. If you cannot name one, the concept probably belongs in the last bulk pass, not this one.
- **Mac-native feasibility**: assume a solo developer on Mac. Flag or avoid anything requiring large art pipelines, online multiplayer, huge authored content, advanced 3D, licensed assets, or complex backend infrastructure.
- **Input model**: keyboard/mouse, trackpad, controller, or a simple hybrid. Keep controls simple.
- **Mastery engine**: name what concretely improves at 10, 50, and 200 plays — not just "gets better at reading opponents."
- **Replayability driver**: interaction, procedural variation, player skill, systems, emergent situations, or challenge modes — not content volume.
- **Scope risk / Implementation risk / Art-content burden**: rate Low / Medium / High. Rate conservatively for a solo Mac-native prototype — when uncertain, rate the higher risk, not the lower one.
- **Soft-decision note**: explicitly state that this is an exploratory candidate, not project truth, and name one concrete thing that would need to happen before it could be taken more seriously (e.g. a specific probe or prototype).

Produce exactly one candidate for the batch/lane you are given. Do not produce extra candidates, alternates, or a list.
