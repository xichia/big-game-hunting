# Sailor and Bupa Whimsical-Life Polish Playtest

Date: 2026-07-07
Build tested: https://xichia.github.io/big-game-hunting/prototypes/sailor-and-bupa-go-to-the-beach/?v=8d6c908
Input: PS4 controller
Tester: Ian Chia
Prototype: Sailor and Bupa: Go to the Beach

## Summary

- The build now feels cozy, chill, and charming.
- It is still somewhat boring and needs more depth.
- Bupa feels more cat-like, but personality can continue to develop.
- Beach presentation is now a good baseline.
- The next opportunity appears to be richer, more identifiable beach points of interest and authored Bupa interactions, not pressure/failure mechanics.

## Raw Playtest Notes

### Overall feel
- Cozy and chill, perhaps a little boring. Needs depth.

### Bupa personality
- Bupa feels more like a real cat, but this can continue to develop.
- Some pauses / interest-point behaviors are readable, but some objects are not identifiable. Ian does not always know what Bupa is investigating.
- Current puddle/tidepool is not especially liked.
- Possible more beach-specific objects:
  - Beach ball
  - Umbrella
  - Deck chair
  - Other kids
  - Mum and dad at the start
- Staying near Bupa does feel affectionate/annoying in the right way, but can still be developed further.

### Whimsy / charm
- “mrrp!” / “meow?” is charming.
- More cute noises would help.
- Sailor's subtle pigtail movement is really cute.
- Bupa makes Ian smile.
- The prototype may actually need more scripted / authored points of interest that Bupa can investigate.

### Beach presentation
- Waterline in motion is way better and now a good baseline.
- Sand/water/dunes/vegetation are also way better and now a good platform.
- Horizontal water artifacts appear gone.
- Shell readability is better, but Ian wants them to be more cartoony pixel shells.
- Tidepool readability is better, but tidepools may not be the right common object. If used, they should perhaps be occasional, larger, and connected to the ocean.

### Collection / play loop
- Low-pressure shell collecting is pleasant.
- The prototype still feels a little empty.
- The game may need more points of interest and possibly some further narrative.
- Single biggest missing ingredient: depth and a bigger variety of objects on the beach.

### Do not change yet / preserve
- No specific preserve notes given.

### Possible next direction
- Brainstorm elements that can be added to the beach.

## Interpretation

- The previous visual polish appears successful enough to use as a baseline.
- The low-pressure direction remains viable, but currently risks becoming shallow.
- The emptiness problem seems tied to lack of identifiable objects and authored interactions rather than lack of hazards.
- Bupa personality is the strongest emotional asset and should remain central.
- Points of interest should become more beach-legible and more characterful.
- Current abstract/natural objects like tidepools may be less effective than recognizable beach-life objects.

## Candidate Next Design Question

What beach objects, characters, and small scripted Bupa investigations can add depth and charm while preserving the low-pressure companion feel?

## Explicit Non-Decisions

- No fail state ratified.
- No rescue mechanic ratified.
- No tide/waterline mechanic ratified.
- No hazard system ratified.
- No source-of-truth update ratified.
- Sailor/Bupa is still exploratory and has not replaced Trail of Embers as the leading candidate.
