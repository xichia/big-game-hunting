TESTER: Ian (self-playtest)

## Ian's playtest notes

1. Did Ian understand where he was, where the beast was, and where safety
   was?
   - Yes. It was intuitive and easy to understand.

2. Did dropping an ember feel like a choice, or just a panic button?
   - Yes. Once the mechanics were understood, dropping an ember read as
     bait, control, and timing.

3. Did darkness feel tense or annoying?
   - It had a good mood.
   - Ian liked that he was subconsciously/automatically memorizing the map.
   - It seemed possible to navigate partly from memory, even in the dark.

4. Did losing feel caused by Ian's decision, or by unreadable pursuit?
   - Once learned, the elements felt predictable.
   - Losing felt attributable to player error.

5. Did Ian want to immediately retry?
   - Yes. He played it twice.

Additional feedback:
- The first version was quite easy.
- Ian is happy with the first prototype.
- Ian is excited to develop more challenges and add difficulty through
  complexity or other challenge design.

## Summary interpretation

The initial probe question leans GREEN:

> Does light-as-tool-and-bait create tense readable stealth decisions, or
> frustrating dark navigation?

The evidence supports readable tension, intuitive stealth decisions, fair
failure, and replay pull. Caveat: difficulty is currently low. This is not
final-game ratification.

## Positive signals

- Spatial legibility was immediate: player, beast, and safe zone were all
  understood without instruction.
- Ember drops were used deliberately (bait/control/timing), not as a panic
  reflex.
- Darkness produced mood and engagement (map-memorization) rather than
  disorientation.
- Losses were attributed to player error, not to unreadable or unfair
  pursuit behavior.
- Strong replay pull — voluntarily played twice.

## Risks / caveats

- Single self-playtest by Ian only; no outside/blind tester data yet.
- Difficulty is currently low — the GREEN read may not hold once the beast
  or map are made harder.
- No data yet on whether the mechanic holds up over repeated play beyond
  two runs, or against a tester without prior knowledge of the design intent.

## Recommended next step

Bounded Challenge Pass 1 — a scoped difficulty/complexity increment to
retest the same probe question under harder conditions, rather than a
broader feature expansion.
