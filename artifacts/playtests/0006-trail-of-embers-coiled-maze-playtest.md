# Playtest Evidence: Trail of Embers Coiled Maze (Level 6)

## Context
A Level 6 larger/tighter maze pass was implemented and mechanically audited. Validation passed for all six levels. This playtest evaluates the subjective feel of Level 6, focusing on fairness, restart cost, ember utility, and progression from the previous level.

## Change under test
- Implementation of Level 6 (Coiled Maze).
- Increased maze size and tighter corridors.

## Playtest procedure
Ian conducted manual playtesting with the following steps:
1. Played Level 6 directly (pressing '6').
2. Restarted Level 6 several times (pressing 'R').
3. Intentionally died near the first third, middle, and late maze to feel the restart cost.
4. Attempted solving the maze with normal ember use.
5. Attempted overusing embers to observe beast behavior fairness.
6. Played Level 5 and advanced naturally into Level 6.

## Ian’s observations
- **Intentional deaths:** Felt in line with the rules already learned and therefore fair.
- **Normal ember use:** Felt like a logical progression from previous levels.
- **Overusing embers:** Does not really help that much; the beast behavior still feels fair.
- **Progression:** Advancing from Level 5 to Level 6 felt like a natural progression.

## Interpretation
- Level 6 appears to preserve fairness despite the increased size and tightness of the maze.
- The restart cost did not immediately feel punitive.
- Normal ember use continues the learned grammar of earlier levels.
- Ember overuse not being very helpful is acceptable and may reinforce intentional use.
- Progression from Level 5 to Level 6 feels natural.
- *Note: This is positive playtest evidence, not source-of-truth ratification.*

## Risks remaining
- Given the increased length, player fatigue might occur on repeated playthroughs, though it did not surface in this focused test.
- The exact balance of ember spawn locations versus maze length may need further tuning if a broader player pool finds it overly difficult.

## Recommendation
Proceed with the Level 6 implementation. No immediate balance or mechanical changes are necessary based on this initial playtest.
