## Concepts

Concept name: The Pulse
One-sentence pitch: A game of silent, collective rhythm where players subtly shift a shared tempo to score points without breaking the beat.
Core player action: Tapping or moving in unison while deciding whether to conform, lead, or disrupt the group's tempo.
Beginner appeal: Joining a shared rhythm is instinctive and requires zero rules explanation.
Mastery source: Subtle temporal manipulation, reading the group's musical psychology, and knowing precisely when to lead vs. follow or disrupt.
Replayability driver: The social "vibe" and trust economy are unique every game; strategies for disruption co-evolve with the group.
Social / watchability hook: Watching a silent, focused battle of coordination is tense and fascinating for spectators who can see the micro-shifts.
Likely failure mode: Collapses into a boring steady beat if disruption is too risky or everyone defers.
Smallest probe: Three players tap a steady 4/4 beat. Player A tries to speed the group up by 5 BPM over one minute without visibly breaking the unison. Does the group hold together or shatter?
Forbidden assumptions avoided: Does not rely on digital-only, tabletop-only, competitive-only, cooperative-only, literal hunting, animals, cards as a primary mechanic, a map or board, combat, or a resource economy.

Concept name: The Thread
One-sentence pitch: A logic-building game where players collectively construct an argument, but secretly vie for control over its final conclusion.
Core player action: Placing a premise or logical connector (If/Then, And, Or, Not) into a shared proof tree.
Beginner appeal: Constructing a simple logical argument is an intuitive social act that anyone can attempt.
Mastery source: Strategic ambiguity, setting logical traps, forcing opponents to contradict their own premises.
Replayability driver: The proposition space is infinite; the group's intellectual style and trust patterns change every session.
Social / watchability hook: Watching a proof build toward its inevitable collapse is intellectually thrilling for observers.
Likely failure mode: Analysis paralysis from players trying to mentally compute the full logical tree before acting.
Smallest probe: Two players debate a simple statement using only premises and logical operators from a shared list. A third player declares the argument "broken" when it becomes incoherent. Can one player force the other to break the logic?
Forbidden assumptions avoided: Does not rely on digital-only, tabletop-only, competitive-only, cooperative-only, literal hunting, animals, cards as a primary mechanic, a map or board, combat, or a resource economy.
